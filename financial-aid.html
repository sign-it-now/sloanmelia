FAFSA Quick Guide
Student Income & Assets
Student assets count at 20% toward EFC. Keep savings in a parent-owned 529 or parent savings account to minimize impact. Student checking/savings accounts are assessed more heavily.
Parent Income
Parent income is the largest factor. The EFC formula assesses parent income at rates from 22‚Äì47%. Lower adjusted gross income through pre-tax contributions (401k, FSA) where possible before filing.
Professional Judgment
If your family's financial situation changed significantly (job loss, medical expenses, divorce), you can request a Professional Judgment review from the financial aid office. They can adjust your EFC manually.
Award Letter Comparison
Compare offers by calculating net cost: Total Cost ‚àí Grants ‚àí Scholarships = Net Cost. Do not count loans as aid. A school with a higher sticker price but more grants may cost less than a "cheaper" school.

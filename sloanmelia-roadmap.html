Financial Aid

Illinois 529 ‚Äî Bright Start Plan Integration

What the app will track and explain
Illinois Bright Start 529 ‚Äî Key Facts for the App

What it is: Illinois Bright Start is the state-sponsored 529 college savings plan. Contributions grow tax-free federally and are deductible on Illinois state taxes up to $10,000/year for single filers and $20,000/year for joint filers. Withdrawals for qualified education expenses are completely tax-free at the federal level.

Qualified expenses the app will track: Tuition and fees, books and supplies, room and board (if enrolled at least half-time), computers and software required for enrollment, and special needs services. Importantly ‚Äî graduate school expenses qualify too, so her 529 can fund her master's and PhD costs.

What the Financial Aid module will do: Track the account balance, show current eligible expenses by school and semester, alert when new contributions are needed to maximize the Illinois tax deduction, explain FAFSA treatment of 529 assets (parent-owned 529 has minimal impact on aid ‚Äî only 5.64% of the account counts toward the Expected Family Contribution), and link directly to the Bright Start portal for contributions.

SECURE 2.0 Act update (2024): Unused 529 funds can now be rolled over into a Roth IRA (up to $35,000 lifetime, subject to annual IRA limits) ‚Äî so there's no risk in overfunding. The app will include this as a planning note in the financial module.

The AI Form Filler ‚Äî How It Actually Works

The most powerful tool in the app
1Ô∏è‚É£ Step One
She Pastes the Form

She copies any college application form, FAFSA section, scholarship application, or any form text and pastes it into the Form Analyzer tool. Or she pastes the URL of the page and the coach fetches it. The coach reads the entire form structure ‚Äî every field, every question, every character limit.

Works with any plain text form. For web-based forms, a URL fetch approach pulls the visible form text.

2Ô∏è‚É£ Step Two
Coach Reads Her Vault

The AI coach cross-references every form field against her Document Vault and Achievements Log. It knows her GPA, her extracurriculars, her transcript data, her volunteer hours, her Mu Alpha Theta membership details, her intended major, her career goals ‚Äî everything she uploaded.

The vault is the secret weapon. The more she uploads, the smarter the coach gets about her specifically.

3Ô∏è‚É£ Step Three
Generates Copy-Paste Output

The coach produces a formatted document ‚Äî field by field ‚Äî with her information pre-filled. Every answer respects the character count limit. She reviews, edits if needed, and copies each answer directly into the form. No retyping the same information for the fifteenth school. No forgetting what she wrote for the last one.

Output format: labeled blocks she can copy one at a time. Also generates a full PDF version she can save as a record of every application she submitted.

4Ô∏è‚É£ Bonus Step
Flags What's Missing

If the form asks for something she hasn't uploaded yet ‚Äî a specific letter of recommendation, a test score she hasn't added, a certification she needs to document ‚Äî the coach flags it and adds it to a checklist. She never submits an incomplete application because she forgot to upload a document.

This checklist-per-school feature directly addresses the most common and costly college application mistake: incomplete submissions.
